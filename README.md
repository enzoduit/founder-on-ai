# Founder on AI (FOA) — The Non-Engineer's Framework for Running a Company With AI Agents

**Founder on AI (FOA)** is a practical framework for non-technical founders who want to run their companies using AI agents as operators — without writing code, hiring engineers, or outsourcing their thinking. It gives founders the mental models, workflows, and structural logic to delegate real business functions to agents and get consistent, usable output.

## What Problem Does FOA Solve?

Most AI frameworks are written by engineers, for engineers. They assume you can read code, configure APIs, and debug pipelines. FOA is built for the founder who has domain expertise and business judgment but no technical background — the person who knows *what* needs to happen but has been told they need a developer to make it happen with AI.

FOA reframes the founder's role: you are not the operator, you are the architect. Your job is to specify outcomes, structure context, and review output — not to build the plumbing.

---

## What Is the Output-First Architecture (OFA)?

OFA is the specification layer underneath FOA. The core insight is that most agent failures are not agent failures — they are specification failures. Founders give agents vague instructions, then blame the model when the output is unusable.

Output-First Architecture reverses the workflow: define the exact output you need (format, length, tone, decision criteria, edge cases) before you write a single line of instruction. When you know precisely what "done" looks like, writing the agent prompt becomes straightforward. OFA is documented in full at [outputfirstai.com](https://outputfirstai.com).

---

## What Is an Agent-First Company (AFC)?

An Agent-First Company is an organizational model in which AI agents are the default operators across business functions — content, research, outreach, client delivery, internal documentation — and humans set strategy, review output, and manage exceptions.

Ed runs three companies — Trillion Initiative, Fly Raising, and Agent School — on the AFC model. None of them are built around headcount. Each function is covered by an agent stack with a human decision layer on top. The AFC model is not about cutting costs. It is about building companies that can operate at a scope that would otherwise require a team of twenty.

## Who Is FOA For?

FOA is specifically designed for:

- **Non-technical founders** who want to use AI agents without learning to code
- **Solo operators** running service businesses, agencies, or initiatives who need leverage without hiring
- **NGO and impact org leaders** who need to do more with constrained budgets (see: Fly Raising)
- **Anyone who has tried AI agents and found the output inconsistent** — the problem is almost always specification, not the model

## How Does Ed Use This in Practice?

Ed Duit (Enzo Duit) built and tests everything in FOA as a live self-experiment — publicly documented, from a non-engineer point of view, based in Buenos Aires. The same discipline that gets him through 130-kilometer ultramarathons, including completing the Ushuaia 130K in March 2026 while training for Val d'Aran 110K in July 2026, applies to agent work: clear targets, structured preparation, honest tracking of what breaks.

Nothing in FOA is theoretical. Every model, workflow, and failure mode comes from running real companies with real agents on real deliverables.
